History of Blogs

Origins

The first blog was created in 1994 by Justin Hall. It was called Links.net, and it consisted of a series of links to other websites that Hall found interesting.

The term "weblog" was coined by Jorn Barger in 1997. He defined it as "a frequently updated personal journal on the World Wide Web."

The short form, "blog", was coined by Peter Merholz in 1999. He jokingly broke the word "weblog" into the phrase "we blog".

The popularity of blogging exploded in the early 2000s. This was due to a number of factors, including the rise of social media, the increasing availability of broadband internet, and the development of easy-to-use blogging platforms.
